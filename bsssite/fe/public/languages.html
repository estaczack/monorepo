The English Language and the Technology Market

Daniel Magno

@magndon

In the rapidly evolving context of technology, the pursuit of continuous updating and improvement is an undeniable necessity for IT professionals in Brazil. Alongside technical skills, mastery of the English language has become an essential tool for success in the industry.

Statistical data reveals that the technology market in Brazil has experienced remarkable growth in recent years. According to research, the sector grew by approximately 9.8% in 2021 and continues to expand. With the adoption of digital transformation across various industries, the demand for qualified IT professionals is high, presenting a range of opportunities for those seeking to excel in this field.

However, one of the main challenges faced by many IT professionals in Brazil is a lack of English fluency. According to statistics, only around 5% of the Brazilian population possesses advanced levels of proficiency in the English language. This scenario represents a significant obstacle to the full development of these professionals' careers.

The scarcity of English-proficient professionals directly impacts job vacancies and opportunities that cannot be adequately filled. Many companies seek talents who can communicate fluently in English, whether it's to interact with international clients, collaborate on global projects, or stay updated on international market trends. Therefore, language proficiency is an important differentiator for securing strategic and valued positions in the Brazilian technology market.

When comparing the Brazilian scenario with other developing countries such as China, India, Indonesia, and South Africa, it becomes apparent that these nations have distinguished themselves by emphasizing English language learning. In these countries, English is widely regarded as a fundamental skill for both professional and personal growth, almost becoming a requirement for IT professionals seeking opportunities in the global market.

Meanwhile, in Brazil, many professionals still struggle to keep up with international demand due to the language barrier. This results in missed opportunities for collaboration with foreign companies, participation in innovative projects, and access to cutting-edge technical and scientific knowledge.

In light of this panorama, it is evident that learning and improving English is a crucial step for IT professionals in Brazil. Those who invest in language fluency will be better prepared to face the challenges of the global technology industry, open doors to international opportunities, and contribute significantly to the country's development. English is not just a language but a powerful tool for professional growth and a lever for achieving success in the competitive technology market.

Daniel Magno

Daniel Magno is an English instructor who lives in São José – Santa Catarina. Active in his brand Magndon since 2017, he advocates for education as the main instrument to combat inequalities, promote diversity, and foster a space for debating current controversial topics.

Daniel began his career in the technology field in 2008, working as a computer technician, IT assistant, programmer, analyst, and systems consultant. However, due to homophobia, he chose to leave this field and venture into another profession, using his passion for the English language, which has always been with him since childhood, and led him to become a language instructor.

His experience with international IT projects and constant contact with foreign suppliers and clients like Qlik, Lufthansa, and RackSpace greatly contributed to his ability to create customized classes focused on conversation. Segments of his English lessons are shared on his social media as "Torture Session," aptly named to break down the fear presented by students when facing native English speakers. The Torture Session can be followed for free on LinkedIn and YouTube using the hashtag #magndonst. These are short videos presenting very useful words and expressions with dual subtitles in English and Portuguese.

Inspired by materials published on social media platforms like Facebook, Instagram, and YouTube, Daniel created his own teaching material. The videos provide support and complement another resource used by Magndon: Ellii - a Canadian provider of didactic material.

Magndon takes pride in being a school run by a gay teacher who turned his room into a classroom via Google Meet meetings. Despite being small, Magndon has already conducted conversation rounds and dubbing sessions with natives from the United States, South Africa, United Kingdom, Australia, and New Zealand.
